# Objective

Recommend a reading list for someone who wants to develop a serious understanding of
the Roman Empire — its rise, operation, culture, and fall.

# Requirements

- Cover the full arc: Republic into Empire, Augustan consolidation, height of the
  Principate, the Crisis of the Third Century, late antiquity and collapse
- Include a mix of ancient primary sources and modern secondary works
- Divide into tiers: essential reads first, then deeper cuts for those who want more
- For each book include: author, title, a one-sentence description of what it covers,
  and why it belongs on the list
- Note any books that are accessible to general readers vs. those that are more
  demanding

# Constraints

- Do not include Gibbon as a primary recommendation (it can appear as an honorable
  mention); focus on books that reflect current scholarship
- Aim for roughly fifteen to twenty titles total
- Prefer books that are in print or widely available
