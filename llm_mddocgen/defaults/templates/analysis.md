Produce an analytical breakdown of the subject. Go beyond summarizing — interpret, reason, and weigh the evidence. The goal is to produce an informed judgment.

Required output sections:
- **# Analysis** — one paragraph framing what is being analyzed and the analytical approach used
- **# Evidence** — the key facts and sources that inform the analysis, organized by sub-topic
- **# Interpretation** — what the evidence means; a reading of the situation grounded in the evidence
- **# Counterarguments** — significant alternative interpretations or objections, addressed directly
- **# Conclusion** — the clearest, most defensible judgment the evidence supports
- **# Confidence and Caveats** — explicit notes on what would change this conclusion if new information emerged

Be direct. State a position. Use hedges only where genuinely warranted by the evidence, not as a default.
