Produce a draft communication ready to send with minimal editing.

Required output sections:
- **# Tone and Purpose Notes** — brief notes on the tone used, the relationship assumed, and the intended effect; explain choices that might be adjusted
- **# Draft** — the full text of the communication, formatted appropriately for the medium (email, letter, message, memo); include subject line if applicable
- **# Alternatives** — 2–3 alternative phrasings for the most consequential part of the message (the ask, the close, or the most sensitive statement)

The draft must have: an opening that establishes context, a body that states the purpose clearly, and a close with a specific ask or next step. Do not write vague closings. The reader should know exactly what is expected of them.
