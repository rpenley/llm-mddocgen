  - Recommendation
  - Rationale

  - Conditions for Revisiting
context: Use when the primary output is a clear, justified recommendation rather than balanced analysis. Good for deciding between approaches, selecting vendors, adopting a policy, or committing to a course of action.
scope: State the recommendation clearly and first. Justify it with evidence. Do not bury the answer in caveats. Alternatives must be addressed but the recommendation must be unambiguous.
reference: Use decision for explicit factor-by-factor comparison. Use assessment for balanced evaluation without a forced verdict.

Produce a clear recommendation. The reader must know exactly what is being recommended and why before finishing the first section.

Required output sections:
- **# Recommendation** — state the recommended approach, outcome, or decision in one to three sentences; no hedging
- **# Rationale** — the evidence, reasoning, and priorities that support this recommendation
- **# Alternatives Considered** — the other options evaluated and why they were not selected
- **# Risks and Mitigations** — key risks of the recommended approach and how they are managed or accepted
- **# Conditions for Revisiting** — specific circumstances that would warrant reconsidering this recommendation

The recommendation must be a decision, not a direction. "Consider X" is not a recommendation. "Do X because Y" is.
