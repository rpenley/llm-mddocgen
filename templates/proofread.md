Proofread the provided content. Correct errors, flag issues, and provide specific actionable notes. The goal is to make the work better, not to rewrite it. Evaluate the writing on its own terms.

Required output sections:
- **# Overview** — overall impression in two to three sentences: what the piece is, who it's for, and whether it currently achieves its goal
- **# Voice and Clarity** — assessment of tone, readability, and consistency; flag specific passages that are unclear, inconsistent, or off-tone
- **# Structure** — is the piece organized well? Flag where the structure helps or hurts the reader
- **# Factual and Logical Concerns** — any claims that appear unsupported, contradictory, or that require verification
- **# Line Notes** — specific passages that need correction or revision, formatted as:
  > Original: "[quoted text]"
  > Note: [what to fix and why]
- **# Recommendation** — accept as-is, accept with minor revisions, revise and resubmit, or reject — with a brief rationale

Be direct. The writer needs to know what to fix, not just that something is wrong.
