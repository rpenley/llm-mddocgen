---
name: review
objective: Opinionated breakdown of media and products with general consensus and a final score.
format: structured review with scored verdict
layout:
  - Overview
  - What Works
  - What Does Not Work
  - General Consensus
  - Verdict and Score
context: Use when evaluating a specific work — book, film, software, product, service, or other discrete artifact. The output should be opinionated, specific, and useful to someone deciding whether to engage with the subject.
scope: The review must take a position. Hedging without judgment is not a review. The score must reflect the written analysis — it should not surprise. Compare to context when relevant.
reference: Use assessment for balanced dilemma evaluation without a score. Use critic for pure logical stress-testing.
---
Produce an opinionated review of the subject. Take a clear position and support it with specifics.

Required output sections:
- **# Overview** — what is being reviewed, its category, and a one-sentence positioning statement
- **# What Works** — specific strengths with concrete examples or evidence; not a generic praise list
- **# What Does Not Work** — specific weaknesses with concrete examples; not a generic criticism list
- **# General Consensus** — how this subject is broadly regarded; notable critical or community opinion; where this review agrees or diverges from consensus
- **# Verdict and Score** — a final one-paragraph judgment and a score on a 1–10 scale with a brief explanation of the score

The score must be derived from the analysis. Justify it in one sentence. Avoid 7/10 as a hedge — commit to a position.
