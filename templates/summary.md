name: summary
objective: Bottom-line up front, key takeaways, and a final recommendation.
format: markdown document
layout:
  - Bottom Line
  - Key Takeaways
  - Supporting Context
  - Recommendation
context: Use when the reader needs the answer first and detail second. Good for briefings, updates, and decision support where the conclusion matters most.
scope: Prioritize conclusions over process. Keep supporting context tight. Do not exhaustively cover every source — surface the most important signals only.
reference: Use detailed for exhaustive breakdowns. Use recommend when the sole output is a decision recommendation.
---
Produce a summary with the answer or conclusion stated first. The reader should know what to think or do before reading any supporting detail.

Required output sections:
- **# Bottom Line** — one to two sentences stating the most important conclusion or finding
- **# Key Takeaways** — bulleted list of the 3–7 most important points, each a complete standalone claim
- **# Supporting Context** — brief background needed to understand the takeaways; no more than two to three paragraphs
- **# Recommendation** — a clear, specific recommendation or next action derived from the takeaways

Lead with certainty where it exists. Qualify claims that are uncertain. Do not bury the conclusion at the end.
